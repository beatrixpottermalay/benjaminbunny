    • Japanese Kimono

    The Japanese kimono is the traditional attire of Japanese women. Japanese people started wearing kimonos during the Heian period.
    There are many types of kimonos. The one with extra long sleeves is called furisode. It is typically worn by unmarried Japanese women.
    A furisode is expensive especially if it is made of silk.

    Types of kimonos

    Here is a simple list of Japanese kimonos:

    1. Furisode
    2. Hikizuri
    3. Tomesode
    4. Houmongi
    5. Iromuji
    6. Komon
    7. Tsukesage
    8. Uchikake
    9. Shiromuku



    Japanese Fans

    Folding fans originated in Japan. It was invented sometime in the 6th to 9th century. In Japanese, it is known as sensu. The Japanese fan can be a decorative item or a hand accessory.

    This Japanese fan is made of shiny paper and wood. As you can see, colourful flowers are painted on the fan.

    This Japanese fan has a Buddhist sutra written on it.

    Hokusai, a famous Japanese artist that lived in the Edo period, created the woodblock print named The Great Wave Off Kanagawa.
    His masterpiece is printed on the leaves of this fan. More information about Japanese woodblock prints can be found on this site.

    This Japanese fan is made of shiny gold coloured paper and bamboo. It is generally regarded as a woman's fan.

    This Japanese fan has an image of a geisha. A geisha is a Japanese female artist and entertainer that specializes in Japanese traditional performing arts.

    This Japanese fan is made of paper and bamboo.
    Some Japanese characters are written on the fan.

    This Japanese fan is made of paper and bamboo. It is smaller in size compared to the Japanese dance fan.

    This Japanese fan is made of polyester cloth and bamboo. The patterns are printed on the cloth.

    Japanese Shikishi

    This is a shikishi board. A shikishi board is used by Japanese people to write messages and given to acquaintances. It is made of wood and it is like a postcard without a stamp and address. Some shikishi boards are larger and these are commonly used as decorative paintings at home.
